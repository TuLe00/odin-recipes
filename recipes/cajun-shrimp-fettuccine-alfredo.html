Cajun Shrimp Fettuccine Alfredo

Home

Decription

Dinner can be on the table in just 20 minutes with this easy shrimp dish. Sweet shrimp, a hint of heat from the Cajun seasoning and red pepper flakes, and creamy fettuccine are perfect for a quick weeknight meal.

ingredients

  • 2 ounces dry fettuccine pasta
  • 1 ½ teaspoons all-purpose flour
  • ¾ teaspoon salt-free Cajun seasoning
  • 5 ounces large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 teaspoons unsalted butter
  • ½ teaspoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon finely grated garlic
  • ¼ cup unsalted chicken stock
  • ⅛ teaspoon white pepper
  • 1 pinch red pepper flakes (Optional)
  • ¼ cup light whipping cream
  • 3 tablespoons freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • salt to taste
  • 1 teaspoon chopped fresh parsley

Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil. Cook fettuccine according to package directions until tender yet slightly firm to the bite, 7 to 8 minutes. Drain, reserving 1/4 cup of pasta water.
  2. Combine flour and Cajun seasoning in a small bowl. Pat shrimp dry, add to the bowl, and toss to coat.
  3. Heat butter and olive oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. When butter bubbles, add shrimp and cook until opaque and pink, about 2 minutes per side. Transfer shrimp to a clean bowl. Add garlic to the skillet and cook until fragrant, about 30 seconds. Add broth to the skillet and scrape up any brown bits.
  4. Reduce heat to medium-low. Season broth with white pepper and red pepper flakes. Slowly whisk in cream and continue cooking for 2 minutes. Add Parmesan cheese slowly, whisking constantly. Cook until sauce has thickened, 2 minutes more. Return shrimp to the skillet, add pasta, and toss to combine.
  5. Add reserved cooking water 1 tablespoon at a time, if sauce is too thick. Season with salt to taste. Serve sprinkled with parsley.
